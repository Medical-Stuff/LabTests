Fasting Blood Sugar
Blood test to check the quantity of Sugar (Glucose: the fuel to every cell in the body) present in the blood sample post 8 hours of fasting, generally overnight fast. This test will help your doctor in understanding the sugar levels in your blood and the way your body utilises sugar while fasting.
Haemoglobin
This is a blood test to determine the amount of Haemoglobin in the blood. Haemoglobin is a complex iron metallic protein which is a combination of Haem a strand to contain iron and Globin a protein to transport haem to the cells. This complex takes up to 4 oxygen molecules to cells and brings back the carbon dioxide to the lungs and gives blood its typical red colour. They are present on the surface of the RBC. This test will help your doctor understand the ability of your blood in exchange of gases.
HBA1c
This is blood test which gives a 3 month average sugar in Hemoglobulin or Blood. Hemoglobulin A1c is a type of protein in Red Blood cell that is responsible for exchange of gases, oxygen to the cells and bring back Carbon dioxide. When sugar builds in the blood it binds to Hemoglobulin. This test determines how much sugar is bound to RBC. Since RBC lives for 3 months it gives 3 months average. This test helps your doctor understand the effectiveness of the sugar control regime he is following and adjust accordingly.
Post Prandial Blood Sugar
Blood test to check the quantity of Sugar (Glucose: the fuel to every cell in the body) present in the blood sample 2 hours post meal. This test will help your doctor in understanding the sugar levels in your blood and the way your body converts food into sugar and post meal.
Serum Amylase
This is a blood test to determine the amount of Amylase in the blood. Amylase is a type of an enzyme (proteins enzyme) present in saliva and pancreas. It is normal to have low levels of amylase in the blood. If it increases beyond the level there could be issues with pancreas or rarely salivary gland. This test will help your doctor understand functioning your pancreas or if you have pancreatitis
Serum Calcium
This is a blood test to determine the amount of Calcium in the blood. Calcium available in the blood is in 3 forms, elemental calcium or free calcium, Calcium bound to proteins, calcium bound to other minerals. Bones, heart, muscles, nerves, kidneys and teeth need calcium for their functioning. This test will help your doctor understand the functioning of muscle, nerves, health of your bones, functioning of parathyroid glands, heart as well as deficiency in Vitamin D.
Serum Cortisol
Serum Cortisol is a blood test to determine the amount of cortisol in your blood. Cortisol is a steroid hormone produced by adrenal gland, situated right above of your kidneys. Cortisol determines your body’s response to external threats. This test primarily help you doctor to understand your behaviour, growth processes, reproductive health and immune system health.
Vitamin B12
This is a blood test to determine the amount of Vitamin B12 in the blood. Vitamin B12, also called cobalamin, is a water-soluble vitamin that plays key role in the normal functioning of the brain and central nervous system as well as in the formation of red blood cells. This test will help your doctor understand your symptoms and associate it with deficiency of this vitamin.
Vitamin D
Vitamin D is a blood test used to determine Vitamin D levels in your blood.Vitamin D is essential for the proper growth and formation of teeth and bones.This test will help your doctor to diagnose bone disease or bone weakness.

Albumin
This is a blood test to determine the total amount of Protein- Albumin present in the blood. Albumin, produced by liver, maintains the consistency of the blood to prevent blood from leaking from blood vessels. This test will help your doctor understand the health of your liver and kidneys. It also helps the doctor suggest you proper diet and check if your body is absorbing all what it needs from the diet.
Bilirubin - Direct
Bilirubin a yellowish substance is produced when liver breaks down old RBC. Bilirubin are of two types , some of the bilirubin gets attached to glucuronic acid and rest remain unattached. The binded one is also called conjugated bilirubin is direct bilirubin and unbinded one is indirect. Bilirubin percolates to the gall bladder and then to the digestive system to throw it out of the body through stools giving stools their typical colour. However a very small amount will remain in blood. This blood test measures the amount of total bilirubin in the blood which will help your doctor understand how well your liver functions.
Bilirubin - Indirect
Test measures the amount of bilirubin in your blood. Your body makes bilirubin when it breaks down hemoglobin, a protein in red blood cells liver removes bilirubin from your body, so measuring it is one way to check how well your liver is working

Chloride
This is a blood test to determine the amount of chloride in the blood. Chloride is a very important electrolyte in the blood which is responsible to maintain the intra and extra cellular fluid balance, blood volume, blood pressure and pH of your body fluids. This test will help your doctor understand your symptoms and correlate with chlorides imbalance.
Potassium
This is a blood test to determine the amount of Potassium in the blood. Potassium works with sodium and helps maintain normal blood and functioning of muscles and nerves. Normally your body maintains the levels of potassium; however its imbalance causes wide range of symptoms. This test will help your doctor understand your symptoms and correlate with potassium imbalance.
PSA for Men
Prostate Specific Antigen is a blood test to determine the amount of Prostate Specific Antigen(PSA)in man’s blood. PSA is a protein produced by prostate gland in males. It observed in the blood in trace quantities which however increases due to various conditions. This test will help doctor to rule out certain conditions.
Serum Iron
Serum Iron is a blood test to determine the amount of Iron in the blood. Iron is essential in the production of Haemoglobin a chemical on the surface of RBC which is responsible for exchange of gases. This test will help your doctor understand the health of your RBC and its efficiency in exchanging gases. It also indicates common problem called anaemia; lack of haemoglobin.
Serum Lipase
This is a blood test to determine the amount of Lipase in the blood. Lipase is an enzyme produced by pancreas which help in breaking fats as well as assist in movement of nutrients and cell waste through the cell membrane. This test will help your doctor understand the health of your pancreas.
Sodium
This is a blood test to determine the amount of sodium in the blood. Sodium is a required for normal blood pressure and functioning of muscles and nerves. Your body gets sodium from food and loses through urine, stools and sweat. Normally your body maintains the levels of sodium; however its imbalance causes wide range of symptoms. This test will help your doctor understand your symptoms and correlate with sodium imbalance.

Blood Urea Nitrogen(BUN)
This is a blood test to measure the amount of Nitrogen content in the blood Urea. Urea is a harmful chemical produced by the liver while breaking down proteins. Urea is removed from the blood by the Kidneys. Levels of blood urea nitrogen will help your doctor understand how your kidneys and liver work.
Routine Urine Analysis (RUA)
Urine Analysis also called as urine routine,is a urine test to screen various common diseases and conditions.This test can detect various diseases and conditions such as kidney disorders,Liver disorders,Metabolic conditions etc.This test will help doctor to associate symptoms with disease.
Serum Creatinine
Creatinine is a waste product of the body which is produced mainly by the muscles. Muscles uses Creatine for energy and in turn produce Creatinine which is taken out by the kidneys through urine. Levels of Creatinine in the blood is measured which will help your doctor understand how your kidneys work.
Serum Uric Acid
This is a blood test to determine the amount of Uric Acid in the blood. Uric Acid is a waste product which is eliminated by our body by the kidneys. Exceptionally high or levels are caused due to improper functioning of kidneys or other reasons. The test will help the doctor understand if you have gout and understand your kidney health as well as your food and water intake habit.

High Density Lipoprotein Cholesterol is termed as “good” cholesterol which removes cholesterol from the circulation and arterial walls and takes it to liver to excrete it out of the body. This test will help your doctor understand how well your body takes out cholesterol.

LDL Cholesterol
This is a blood test to check the amount of LDL Cholesterol in your blood. Low Density Lipoprotein Cholesterol is termed as “bad” cholesterol since it is end product of VLDL converting to IDL cholesterol which further converts to LDL cholesterol. Since most of the triglycerides are gone it becomes purer cholesterol which is sticky and form plaques around arterial walls. This test will help your doctor understand your risk if getting plaque related issues such as coronary heart diseases, strokes, deep vein thrombosis etc.
